$$\eqalign{
\\{gloss\ construction}\longrightarrow\;&
   \hbox{\tt\backslash begingl}\\{\it spaces}\\{item\ list}\hbox{\tt\backslash endilg}
   \\{free\ translation}\hbox{\tt\backslash endgl}\cr
\\{item\ list}\longrightarrow\;& \\{item}\\{\it spaces} \;\vert\;
   \\{item}\\{\it spaces}\\{item\ list}\cr
\\{item} \longrightarrow\;&
   \\{body}\\{optional\ suffix}\\{optional\ diacritic}\cr
\\{body} \longrightarrow\; &
   \\{\it word\ item}\ttlbrack \\{gloss}\ttrbrack\cr
\\{gloss} \longrightarrow \;&
   \\{\it gloss\ item}\\{secondary\ gloss}\cr
\\{secondary\ gloss} \longrightarrow \;&
   \emptyset
   \;\vert\; \ttslash \\{\it gloss\ item}\\{secondary gloss}\cr
\\{optional\ suffix}\longrightarrow\;&
    \emptyset \;\vert\; \ttlbrace \\{\it suffix\ item} \ttrbrace\cr
\\{optional\ diacritic}\longrightarrow\;&
    \emptyset \;\vert\; \ttplus\cr
\\{free\ translation}\longrightarrow\;&
   \emptyset\;\vert\;
   \hbox{\tt \backslash glft}\\{\,\it free\ translation\ stuff}\ttslash\ttslash\cr
}$$
The terms above in italics are from the standard Tex syntax, with
some restrictions.  $\\{\it spaces}$ is any nonempty sequence of
spaces and |\par| tokens.  {\it word items\/}, {\it gloss
items\/}, and {\it suffix items} must not contain top-level
spaces.  Spaces in these items must be hidden inside groups. {\it
word items\/} and {\it gloss items\/} must be able to go in an
hbox.  {\it suffix items\/} and the {\it free translations
stuff\/} must not have explict |\par| tokens, although they can
contain implicit |\par| tokens, |\endgraf| in particular.

On the printing end, each $\\{body}$ contributes a vbox to the
line under construction, with the word and gloss items stacked
one on top of the other.  $\\{\it suffix\ item}$ contributes its
contents.  $\\{\it optional\ diacritic}$ contributes
|\hskip\glspace| if it is missing and nothing if it is |+|.

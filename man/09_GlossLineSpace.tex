\subsection Vertical spacing in the interlinear gloss

This section will describe only the basics, assuming the default
setting of some parameters (the boolean parameters |autoglskip| and
|glstruts|) which are not discussed here.

Consider a simple interlinear gloss like (\nextx).

\ex[glspace=1.5em]
\begingl
\gla Ti ma'a'\~nao hao kumuentusi
   ni h\'ayiyi ha'.//
\glb not agr.afraid you
   Infin.speak.to not anyone Emp//
\endgl
\xe
{\it Expex\/} builds this gloss as illustrated in (\nextx).  The
sequence of boxes is is generated and fed into {\it Tex\/}'s paragraph
building machinery.   Struts are put on each line of each of the
boxes, which are called {\it glwords}.  The spaceskip is adjusted to
be the value of |glspace|.

\ex[glspace=1.5em]  \boxglwords %\glboxed
\begingl
\gla Ti ma'a'\~nao hao kumuentusi
   ni h\'ayiyi ha'.//
\glb not agr.afraid you
   Infin.speak.to not anyone Emp//
\endgl
\xe
Problems may arise if one has exceptionally tall or deep characters,
or one wants line spacing inside glwords to differ from line spacing
outside glwords.  We put these issues off for the time being.

Wrapping introduces another layer of complexity. In (\nextx), the line
spacing internal to glwords is different than the spacing of the lines
of glwords.

Now, in addition to line spacing inside glwords, the vertical spacing
of the lines of glwords must be specified.

The baseline of the glwords is the baseline of the top line.

\ex
\hsize=5in
\begingl[everyglc=\tenrm,aboveglcskip=-2pt,glspace=!.5em,
   extraglskip=1ex,aboveglftskip=0pt]
\gla Mary$_i$ ist sicher, dass es den Hans nicht st\"oren w\"urde
seiner Freundin ihr$_i$ Herz auszusch\"utten.//
\glb Mary is sure that it the Hans not annoy would
his girlfriend her heart {out to throw}//
\glc {} {} {} {} {} DAT {} {} {} {} DAT DAT ACC ACC //
\glft  `Mary is sure that it would not annoy John to reveal her
heart to his girlfriend.'//
\endgl
\xe
